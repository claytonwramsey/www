This is where I keep my recipes. If you make one, feel free to email me a picture and tell me how it tastes.

I live in Houston, which is famously humid.
You may need to reduce the amount of flour that you add to most baking recipes to reach a desired moisture level.

My oven is finicky and refuses to keep a consistent temperature.
I also suspect that it does not actually reach its stated temperatures, so you may want to reduce your own oven temps if you have a good oven.
Instead of religiously following my bake timings, I instead suggest setting your timer for a few minutes early, then regularly peeking until you're sure it's done.

For anything except flour, water, and oil, I always eyeball my quantities.
With vanilla extract, I recommend just giving a generous pour straight from the bottle.
When I add garlic to a recipe, I go until I think "that's about enough garlic," then double it.
