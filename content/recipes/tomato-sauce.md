My mother's recipe for tomato sauce, featuring my own modifications.
It's quite hearty, and, more importantly, quite cheap.

This recipe makes about 8 to 10 servings.
It takes roughly 1 hour to make.

## Ingredients

- Olive oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 bell peppers, chopped
- 2 tbsp garlic, minced
- 1 lb ground hot Italian sausage
- 32 oz canned diced tomatoes
- 16 oz canned tomato sauce
- 6 oz canned tomato paste
- Salt
- Pepper
- Oregano
- Basil
- Other herbs and spices to taste (e.g. rosemary, thyme, bay leaves)

## Directions

1.  Heat a large pot on the stove. Sauté onion, celery, bell
    peppers, and garlic in oil until clear.
2.  Add sausage, slicing with a spatula until mixed and mostly cooked,
    approx. 5 min.
3.  Add tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste. Bring to a simmer.
4.  Add seasonings to taste.
5.  Simmer until the sauce has a thick, unctuous texture.
    I recommend forgetting about the sauce for at least 20 minutes, but after an hour it gets even better.
6.  Serve over pasta.

## Notes

- Use whatever vegetables you like in this recipe. I don't like black
  olives, but you can definitely add them if you like them.
- My mother's version originally called for 4 sticks of celery.
  I don't particularly like it that way, but you can add them if it makes you feel more authentic.
